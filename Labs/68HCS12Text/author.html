About the Author

Tom Almy has written assembly language programs for the Freescale 68HC11, 68HC12, 68HC16, 68000, and 6800, Intel 80x86, 8048, 8051, and Zilog Z-80 microcontrollers and microprocessors, as well as minicomputers and mainframes. Microcontroller and microprocessor programs have included stepper and servo motor controllers, floppy disk drivers, keyboard controllers, data analysis, and implementation of Forth language interpreters.

Tom holds eight patents, most pertaining to digital and analog circuit designs.

Tom is currently a Senior Design Engineer at Veris Industries, designing environmental sensors and controls, using a variety of microcontrollers. Previously he was a Principal Design Engineer at Credence Systems for eight years, where he designed integrated circuits for automated test equipment. Prior to Credence, he was employed by at Tektronix for 25 years, involved with ASIC design and testing, and processor design.

At Oregon Institute of Technology, since 1990, he has taught C and advanced C programming, ASIC design, Digital Systems, and the Microcontroller and Microprocessor Systems courses, and has been a senior project advisor. He has also taught Forth and Basic programming.

Tom received a MSEE degree from Stanford University in 1973, and a BS with Distinction from Cornell University in 1971.
