Question

The text shows how the SPI can be interfaced to multiple devices, with an effective shift register length a multiple of 8 bits. However could the SPI be interfaced to a single 4 bit device? If so, explain how, and if not, explain why not.

Answer

Yes it can. If shifting least significant bit first (LSBFE=1), then the first four bits shifted out will be shifted through the device and back into the microcontroller. The last four bits shifted out will be captured by the device. The first four bits shifted into the microcontroller will be from the device. So the transmitted data from the microcontroller needs to be in the upper four bits and the received data from the device will appear in the lower four bits. If LSBFE=0, the reasoning is the same, but the data from the microcontroller will be in the lower four bits and the data to the microcontroller will be in the upper four bits.
